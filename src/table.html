--- layout: layouts/base.njk title: Data table example permalink: /data-table/ templateEngineOverride: md,njk --- ## Reimbursement rates The Department pays foster care maintenance reimbursements beginning on the date the department places children or youth in a licensed or certified foster home and each night children or youth reside in the home. Rates vary based both on child or youth age and their needs as diagnosed by a licensed health care provider. All children or youth placed in foster care receive the basic foster care maintenance payment for their age category. There are six additional levels of supplemental payments, for a total of 7 levels.

Level 1: Children or youth are developing comparably to children or youth in the same age range. Level 2: Children or youth require more support from foster parents due to the youth being 12 years of age or older. The payment is based on the youth's needs, who are developing comparably to youth in the same age range. Level 3: Children or youth are diagnosed with at least one chronic medical condition that requires more support from foster parents due to the severity of their needs. Level 4: Children and youth are diagnosed with a developmental disability that requires more support from foster parents due to the severity of their behaviors and needs. Level 5: Children and youth are diagnosed with a developmental disability and have at least one chronic health condition. Level 6: Have a current mental health diagnosis or current prescription medication to assist with their mental health diagnosis. Level 7: Have complex mental health needs and need more support to help stabilize and keep the child or youth safe.
