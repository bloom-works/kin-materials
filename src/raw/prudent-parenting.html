--- layout: layouts/raw.njk title: Prudent parenting templateEngineOverride: md,njk parent_page: Kinship caregiver rules parent_page_link: rules.html --- As a caregiver, you will need to decide whether to allow your kin child to take part in typical childhood activities. To make these decisions, follow prudent parenting standards. Prudent parenting means:  * Using careful and thoughtful decision making. * Prioritizing your kin child’s health, safety, and best interests.  * Encouraging your kin child’s emotional and developmental growth. Use prudent parenting to decide whether to let your kin child take part in:  * Recreational activities or hobbies outside of school * Social activities * Overnight activities where you are not present over 24 hours and up to 72 hours, like school trips  If DCYF or the court created a safety plan, follow the plan when you decide what to allow your kin child to do. {% Callout "related", "Related content" %} * Supervision and monitoring * Transportation * Education * Visistation and family time {% endCallout %} {% Callout "note", "Read the regulations" %} For more information read the Washington Administrative Codes (WACs) related to these topics: * WAC 110-149-0210: Participating in activities {% endCallout %}
