--- layout: layouts/raw.njk title: Visitation and family time templateEngineOverride: md,njk parent_page: Kinship caregiver rules parent_page_link: rules.html --- Family visits can help preserve your child’s bond with their birth family. It can also help your child cope with the emotional challenges of being separated from their birth family. Make your child available for time with their family members and visits with their siblings, according to their case plan.  Allow people who are involved in planning and supporting your child’s wellbeing to meet with your child. These people should be able to meet with your child at any time, including at your home and property:  * DCYF workers * Representatives from the child-placing agency * Representatives from your child’s tribe * Your child’s guardian ad litem (GAL) * Your child’s court appointed special advocate (CASA) * Your child’s attorney {% Callout "related", "Related content" %} * Supervision and monitoring * Discipline and restraint{% endCallout %} {% Callout "note", "Read the regulations" %} For more information read the Washington Administrative Codes (WACs) related to these topics: * WAC 110-149-0130 Meeting the safety and well-being needs of children and youth {% endCallout %}
