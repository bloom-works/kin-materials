--- layout: layouts/rawAllCaregivers.njk title: How to request respite care templateEngineOverride: md,njk --- Respite care can happen at any time. You have to tell the caseworker whenever you plan to use respite care, even if DCYF is not paying the respite caregiver.  If you want DCYF to pay the respite caregiver, send your request for respite care as soon as you know you need it, ideally at least 14 days in advance. It’s especially important to send your request early if you want respite care during a busy time, such as school holidays or breaks.  If you are asking DCYF to find a respite care provider, remember that there is no guarantee that DCYF will be able to find someone. It may be helpful to have someone you know prepared to provide respite if need be. Learn more about how they can become a respite care provider [**add link**]. Whether you are requesting paid respite care or sharing a plan for unpaid respite, follow this process: * **Email all of these people:** * The caseworker * The regional placement desk [**add link**] * Your CPA (if you’re licensed by a CPA) * Your [Caregiver Supports](https://dcyf.wa.gov/services/foster-parenting/caregiver-supports-project/phase-two) placement support manager (if you have one) * **Include this information**: * Your name and contact information * The dates you are planning to use respite care * Whether you are requesting respite to meet your own needs or the needs of a child or youth in your care (for example, if you are requesting respite for one child so that you can take another child to the doctor) * Any special needs, such as medical or behavioral needs * Any appointments or family time visits the child or youth has scheduled during the dates you’re requesting * If you have someone lined up to provide respite care, include their name and contact information and note whether they are certified. * If you plan to trade respite [**add link**] with another caregiver, include any plans that you and the other caregiver have agreed on. * **Send reminder emails** to the people you included in your first email, copying anyone you have lined up to provide respite. This can help make sure that everyone involved is clear on the details.  If your plan does not meet respite care requirements, the caseworker will let you know and work with you to make an updated plan.
