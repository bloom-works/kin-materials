--- layout: layouts/raw.njk title: Reporting serious incidents permalink: /raw/reporting-incidents.html templateEngineOverride: md,njk parent_page: Kinship caregiver rules parent_page_link: rules.html --- You need to report serious incidents involving your child to DCYF. To report a serious incident, call the child’s caseworker. If the child's or youth's caseworker is not available, or it is after normal business hours, call department intake at [1-800-301-1868](tel:+18003011868). If you do not report these events, DCYF will send you a notice of violation. If the violation results in harm, CPS may also open an investigation. {% Callout "note", "Note" %}The reporting time frames listed on this page are meant to give you time to respond to the situation first. **If you need help during an active emergency, call [911](tel:+911)**.{% endCallout %} ### Abuse, harm, or death **Immediately** contact the department intake at [1-800-301-1868](tel:+18003011868) if your child: * Dies or is near death * Has been physically or sexually abused, neglected, or exploited (or if you suspect they have been) * Has had sexual contact with another child or youth that is not consensual or age or developmentally appropriate ### Physical restraint **Within 24 hours**, report behavior that resulted in physical restraint to your child’s caseworker. This includes restraining a child to save them from harm. ### Medical emergencies **Within 48 hours**, report medical emergencies to your child’s caseworker or the department intake. Examples include: * Medication given or taken wrong * Serious illness or injury * Disclosed suicidal or homicidal thoughts, gestures, or attempts * Emergency medical treatment * Emergency psychiatric care {% Callout "related" %} * [Healthcare and medicine](healthcare-meds.html) * [Drugs and alcohol](#) * [Weapons](#) * [Discipline and restraint](#) * [Supervision and monitoring](#) {% endCallout %} {% Callout "note", "Read the regulations" %} For more information read the Washington Administrative Codes (WACs) related to these topics: * WAC 110-149-0290: Reporting incidents {% endCallout %}
