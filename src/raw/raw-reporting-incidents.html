This allows DCYF to offer support or contact necessary authorities. To report a serious incident, contact the child’s caseworker or call the department intake at [1-800-301-1868](tel:+18003011868). {% Callout "note" %}If you do not report these events, DCYF will send you a notice of violation. If the violation results in harm, CPS may also open an investigation.{% endCallout %} ### Abuse, harm, or death **Immediately** contact the department intake if your child: * Dies * Is near death * Has been physically or sexually abused, neglected, or exploited (or if you suspect they have been) ### Physical restraint Contact the child’s caseworker to report behavior that resulted in physical restraint. You must report this **within 24 hours**. This includes restraining a child to save them from harm. ### Medical emergencies Contact the child’s caseworker or the department intake to report medical emergencies. You must report these **within 48 hours**. Some examples include: * Medicine given or taken wrong * Serious illness or injury, or psychiatric care * Disclosed suicidal or homicidal thoughts, gestures, or attempts * Emergency medical treatment * Mental health care ### Sexual contact or behavior Contact the child’s caseworker or the department intake to report concerning sexual contact or sexual behaviors by or towards the child. You must report these **within 48 hours**. Some examples include: * Non-consensual sexual contact * Sexual contact that is not age or developmentally appropriate * Sexual behavior that is not age or developmentally appropriate, such as flashing or public masturbation {% Callout "related" %} - [Healthcare and medicine](healthcare-meds.html) - [Drugs and alcohol](#) - [Weapons](#) - [Discipline and restraint](#) - [Supervision and monitoring](#) {% endCallout %} {% Callout "note", "Read the regulations" %} For more information read the Washington Administrative Codes (WACs) related to these topics: - WAC 110-149-0290: Reporting incidents {% endCallout %}
