--- layout: layouts/raw.njk title: Report health and safety incidents permalink: /raw/reporting-incidents.html templateEngineOverride: md,njk parent_page: Kinship caregiver rules parent_page_link: rules.html --- You need to report serious health and safety incidents involving your child to DCYF. If the child or youth is an enrolled member of a tribe, or eligible to become an enrolled member, you also need to report incidents to the tribe. {% Callout "note", "Note" %} The reporting time frames listed on this page are meant to give you time to respond to the situation first. **If you need help during an active emergency, call [911](tel:+911)**. {% endCallout %} ### Abuse, harm, or death **Immediately** contact the department intake at [1-866-363-4276](tel:+18663634276) if your child: * Dies or is near death * Has been [physically or sexually abused, neglected, or exploited](https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=26.44.020) (or if you suspect they have been) * Has had sexual contact with another child or youth that is not consensual or age or developmentally appropriate ### Physical restraint **Within 24 hours**, report behavior that [resulted in physical restraint](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/discipline-and-restraint) to your child’s caseworker. This includes restraining a child to save them from harm. If you're not sure what physical restraint is, talk to the caseworker. If you don't have your caseworker's contact information, [search our directory](https://apps-ca-ext.dcyf.wa.gov/EmployeeDirectoryInternet/). If the caseworker is not available or it is after normal business hours, call department intake at [1-866-363-4276](tel:+18663634276). ### Medical emergencies **Within 48 hours**, report medical emergencies to your child’s caseworker. Examples include: * Medication given or taken wrong * Serious illness or injury (for example, the child or youth went to the hospital, they broke a bone, or they needed stitches) * Disclosed suicidal or homicidal thoughts, gestures, or attempts * Emergency medical treatment * Emergency psychiatric care If you don't have the caseworker's contact information, [search our directory](https://apps-ca-ext.dcyf.wa.gov/EmployeeDirectoryInternet/). If the caseworker is not available or it is after normal business hours, call department intake at [1-866-363-4276](tel:+18663634276). {% Callout "note", "Note" %} You may experience a long wait time when you call department intake. If possible, we recommend you have a close relative or friend wait on the phone while you respond to the child or youth’s medical emergency. {% endCallout %} {% Callout "related", "Related content" %} * [](healthcare-meds.html)[](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/what-do-i-have-to-report/)[Health and medicine](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/healthcare-meds) * [Discipline and restraint](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/discipline-and-restraint)[](#) {% endCallout %} {% Callout "note", "Read the regulations" %} For more information read the Washington Administrative Codes (WACs) related to these topics: * WAC 110-149-0280: Physical restraint * WAC 110-149-0290: Reporting incidents {% endCallout %}
