--- layout: layouts/raw.njk title: Payments and reimbursements templateEngineOverride: md,njk parent_page: Kinship caregiver rules parent_page_link: rules.html --- If you’re a licensed kinship caregiver, you’ll get monthly foster care maintenance payments while caring for the child or youth.  You can also talk to your child or youth’s caseworker about other financial support to help you meet the child or youth’s needs, regardless of whether you’re licensed or not. This support includes but is not limited to:  * Money to cover the costs of gas, and transportation * Money to cover the costs of property damage  * Vouchers to buy clothes for the child or youth, if you cannot get what the child or youth needs with the monthly foster care maintenance payment, or through local community resources Learn more about [your options and how to get financial support](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/payments). {% Callout "related", "Related content" %} * Payments and financial support * Food and clothing * Transportation * License types {% endCallout %} {% Callout "note", "Read the regulations" %} For more information read the Washington Administrative Codes (WACs) related to these topics: * WAC 110-149-0070: Training, services, and financial support * WAC 110-149-0120: Meeting the basic and specific needs of children and youth * WAC 110-149-0320: Issuing initial licenses * WAC 110-149-0350: Issuing or reassessing kinship licenses {% endCallout %}
