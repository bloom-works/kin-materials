--- layout: layouts/raw.njk title: Indian children templateEngineOverride: md,njk parent_page: Kinship caregiver rules parent_page_link: rules.html --- Follow these guidelines to help your child maintain their culture, and their familial and ancestral ties. ### Tribal connections Provide opportunities for your child to connect with their tribal families and communities including. These opportunities include: * Attending cultural and spiritual events and ceremonies. * Participating in cultural and spiritual other activities. * Learning tribal stories and songs. ### Working with your child’s caseworker Your child’s caseworker is here to support you and your child.   Work with your child’s caseworker to fulfill these requirements:  * Support your child’s connections to their tribal families and communities.  * Repair, replace, or provide culturally significant items for your child.  * Develop a case plan for your child that includes instructions for, but not limited to: * Following recommendations from the local Indian child welfare advisory committee. * Managing payments from a tribe. These payments include per capita, general welfare, and settlement funds. * Allowing your child to attend cultural events and activities.  * Other actions listed on this page ### Health, medicine, and food Work with your child’s caseworker to create a case plan that includes having and using Native American traditional medicines. These medicines include, but are not limited to: * Sage * Cedar * Sweet grass Also work with the caseworker to include these in the case plan:   * Using baby boards and moss bags. * Providing plant-based or lactose free alternatives to cow's milk, if your child is lactose intolerant. ### Discipline Do not interfere with your child’s cultural needs when you discipline your child. For example, do not cut your child’s hair, or take a religious or cultural item or event away from your child. ### Travel If your child is in the care and custody of a tribal court, consult with tribes before your child travels out of the state or country.  Work with your child’s caseworker to include this in your child’s case plan. ### Changing your child’s appearance Get permission from your child’s caseworker before you make changes to your child’s appearance. Changes that you need the caseworker’s permission for include:  * Haircuts * Changes to the color of your child’s hair  * Piercings * Tattoos {% Callout "related", "Related content" %} * LGBTQ+ * Race, ethnicity, religion, and culture * Discipline and restraint * Food and clothing * What do I need permission for? {% endCallout %} {% Callout "note", "Read the regulations" %} For more information read the Washington Administrative Codes (WACs) related to these topics: * WAC 110-149-0140: Additional requirements for Indian children {% endCallout %}
