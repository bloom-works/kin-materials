--- layout: layouts/rawAllCaregivers.njk title: Respite care and other breaks from caregiving templateEngineOverride: md,njk --- {% Callout "note", "Note" %} This information is for caregivers of children and youth in DCYF’s custody only. If a Tribe has placed a child or youth with you, contact the Tribe, or the Tribal caseworker, for more information on respite care and other breaks from caregiving. {% endCallout %} It’s important for caregivers to have support with caregiving. Taking breaks from caregiving can help you prevent burnout, cope with challenges, and be there for children and youth. There are several ways you can have another caregiver temporarily care for children or youth, including: [**Add jump links**] * Respite care  * Child care * Prudent parenting  This page explains what each option is and includes links to more information about each. All these kinds of temporary care are sometimes referred to as “substitute care.” Each option can work a little differently on a case by case basis, so the most important thing is to communicate with the caseworker. They can help you figure out which option makes the most sense for your situation. The [Caregiver Supports](https://dcyf.wa.gov/services/foster-parenting/caregiver-supports-project/phase-two) team and your CaRES mentor [**link to updated page**] can also work with you to find the right support. For more information about Caregiver Supports, visit the [Caregiver Supports website](https://dcyf.wa.gov/services/foster-parenting/caregiver-supports-project/phase-two) or email [dcyf.caregiversupports@dcyf.wa.gov](mailto:dcyf.caregiversupports@dcyf.wa.gov). It’s very helpful to have a strong network of other caregivers, friends, and family who can support you and help care for children and youth. Learn more about community and group support [**link to new page**] where you can build these kinds of connections. {% Callout "note", "Note" %} You must get approval from the caseworker before leaving a child or youth at home alone. {% endCallout %} ## Respite care  Respite care is temporary care that the Department of Children, Youth, and Families (DCYF) can pay for. Anytime you need substitute care that will last for more than 72 hours or happen on a regular basis, you have to use respite care. A respite care provider can be someone you already know or a caregiver that DCYF finds for you. Learn more about respite care [**link to updated page**] and how to arrange it. ## Child care Child care usually refers to situations where a child is going to a licensed child care facility on a regular basis, like daycare. DCYF can often pay for child care. Learn more about [child care](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/child-care/). ## Prudent parenting Prudent parenting refers to the idea that children and youth should be able to participate in typical childhood activities without involving the caseworker. The goal is to help children and youth have normal experiences that are similar to their peers who are not experiencing foster care. Caregivers can decide what activities are in the child or youth’s best interest, including spending occasional time away from the primary caregiver.  For example, if you give a child permission to go to a birthday party or on a weekend trip with a friend’s family, those activities fall under prudent parenting. [Learn more about prudent parenting](https://dcyf.wa.gov/sites/default/files/pubs/CWP_0078.pdf). Occasional babysitting may be considered prudent parenting as well. Learn more about the rules around babysitting. [**Link to WAC page**]
