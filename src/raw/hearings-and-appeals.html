--- layout: layouts/raw.njk title: Hearings and appeals templateEngineOverride: md,njk parent_page: Kinship caregiver rules parent_page_link: rules.html --- If we deny, suspend, or cancel your kinship caregiver license, and you disagree with our decision, you have the right to an administrative hearing.  You must send a written request for a hearing to the office of administrative hearings. Send your written request within 28 days of receiving a certified letter with our decision about your license.  {% Callout "note", "Note" %}If you applied for your license through a child-placing agency and you do not agree with a CPA’s decision on your application, you must follow the CPA’s appeals process to challenge the decision.{% endCallout %} If you did not pass a DCYF [background check](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/background-checks/), and you disagree with our decision, you can also request an administrative hearing. But you cannot appeal convictions, negative actions, or pending criminal charges at the hearing. {% Callout "related", "Related content" %} * Background checks * CPAs and tribal CPAs {% endCallout %} {% Callout "note", "Read the regulations" %} For more information read the Washington Administrative Codes (WACs) related to these topics: * WAC 110-149-0110: Applicants or licensed kinship caregivers' right to an administrative hearing {% endCallout %}
