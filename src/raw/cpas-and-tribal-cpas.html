--- layout: layouts/raw.njk title: CPAs and tribal CPAs templateEngineOverride: md,njk parent_page: Kinship caregiver rules parent_page_link: rules.html --- You can apply for a kinship license through: * DCYF * A child-placing agency (CPA) or a tribal CPA If you apply through a CPA, the CPA will decide whether to certify you.   CPAs and tribal CPAs may have other requirements you need to meet. Any other requirements will be directly related to the child or youth’s needs or case plans.  If you do not agree with an agency’s decision on your application, you must follow the agency’s appeals process to challenge the decision. Review [the list of CPAs and tribal CPAs for kinship licensing](https://www.dcyf.wa.gov/sites/default/files/pubs/LIC_0147.pdf). {% Callout "note", "Note" %} There are [additional rules](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1iAEpiy3c87yIjoEm4SbtH-r_tZ8tiVYR3Y125IyDGPw/edit?tab=t.2cjvvzslvoqy) if you work for a CPA or tribal CPA, or if you have financial ties to a CPA or tribal CPA. {% endCallout %} {% Callout "related", "Related content" %} * [License requirements](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/license-requirements/) * [Licensing special circumstances](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/licensing-special-circumstances/) {% endCallout %} {% Callout "note", "Read the regulations" %} For more information read the Washington Administrative Codes (WACs) related to these topics: * WAC 110-149-0040: Licensing options {% endCallout %}
