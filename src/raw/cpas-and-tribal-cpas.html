--- layout: layouts/raw.njk title: CPAs and tribal CPAs templateEngineOverride: md,njk parent_page: Kinship caregiver rules parent_page_link: rules.html --- You can apply for a kinship caregiver license through: * DCYF * A child-placing agency (CPA) * A tribal CPA If you apply through a CPA, the CPA will decide whether to certify you.   CPAs may have other requirements you need to meet. Any other requirements will be directly related to the child or youth’s needs or case plans.  If you do not agree with a CPA’s decision on your application, you must follow the CPA’s appeals process to challenge the decision. {% Callout "note", "Note" %}There are [additional rules](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1GOCSLOHaC7HCQhuO2ZmC45pCHyPjyA-6qha6f7d9LHA/edit?tab=t.2cjvvzslvoqy) if you work for a CPA, or if you have financial ties to a CPA.{% endCallout %} {% Callout "related", "Related content" %} * License requirements * Licensing special circumstances {% endCallout %} {% Callout "note", "Read the regulations" %} For more information read the Washington Administrative Codes (WACs) related to these topics: * WAC 110-149-0040: Licensing options {% endCallout %}
