--- layout: layouts/rawAllCaregivers.njk title: Who can be a respite care provider templateEngineOverride: md,njk --- Respite care providers support children, youth, and their caregivers by taking care of children and youth for short periods of time.  Respite care gives full-time caregivers time and support to take care of themselves and attend to other necessary responsibilities. It’s an important way to help caregivers avoid burnout and keep children and youth in stable living situations. Anyone over the age of 18 can apply to become a certified respite care or kinship respite provider. Here are a few common scenarios:   * **You’re a licensed foster parent** and you want to keep supporting a child or youth who used to be placed with you. * **You know a child, youth, or family** who is eligible for respite care \[link to respite care page] and you want to support them.  * **You’re interested in becoming a foster parent** or supporting children and youth who are in DCYF’s care. Learn more about certification requirements \[link to new page] and whether they apply to you. You can get a small payment for providing respite care.
