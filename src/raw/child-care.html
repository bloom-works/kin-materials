--- layout: layouts/rawAllCaregivers.njk title: Child care templateEngineOverride: md,njk --- The Department of Children, Youth, and Families (DCYF) can often pay for a child to go to a licensed child care provider, like a daycare. If you want to send a child to a licensed child care provider, start by talking to the caseworker. You may qualify for a referral to have DCYF pay for child care. Caregivers generally qualify for a referral based on work or continuing education schedules. If you know that you will need child care in order to have a child placed with you, it’s best to ask for a referral before you accept the placement. That way, child care can start as early in the placement as possible. Child care is one of several kinds of substitute care that you can use when you need a break. Learn more about all the options for substitute care, including babysitting and respite care. {% Callout "note", "Note" %} If you do not get a referral or if you cannot find a provider that accepts DCYF payments, you are allowed to pay for child care yourself. DCYF cannot reimburse you for child care that you pay for yourself. {% endCallout %} ## How to find a child care provider Once you know you are eligible for a referral, the next step is to find a licensed child care provider that accepts DCYF payments. The caseworker will make the referral for DCYF payment after you choose a provider. To find child care near you, contact Child Care Aware of Washington for a list of licensed providers that match your needs, which may include locations, hours, rates, or types of care. You can visit the [Child Care Aware of Washington website](https://childcareawarewa.org/) or call 1-800-446-1114. You can also use [DCYF’s Child Care Check tool](https://www.dcyf.wa.gov/services/earlylearning-childcare/child-care-check). Talk to the caseworker if you have questions about finding a provider. ## In-home child care If a child who is placed with you has medical or behavioral needs that cannot be met at a standard child care facility, DCYF may pay for them to get child care at home.  In-home child care is approved on a case-by-case basis. If you are interested in in-home child care, talk to the caseworker about starting the approval process.
