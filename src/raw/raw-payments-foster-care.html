--- layout: layouts/raw.njk title: Monthly payments for licensed caregivers permalink: /raw/payments-foster-care.html templateEngineOverride: md,njk parent_page: Payments and financial support parent_page_link: /payments.html --- As a licensed foster parent or licensed kinship caregiver, you can get monthly foster care payments to reimburse you for food, clothing, shelter, and other caregiving expenses. {% Callout "note", "undefined" %} Foster care payments are sometimes called other names, including: foster care maintenance payments (FCMPs), foster care reimbursements, or caregiver support resource level payments. {% endCallout %} ### Eligibility You're eligible for foster care payments if you're: * A licensed foster parent, or * A licensed kinship caregiver {% Callout "note", "Note" %} There are other types of financial support available, **even if you’re not licensed**. For example, DCYF may provide clothing vouchers, and reimbursement for transportation to certain activities.  [Learn about other types of financial support for all caregivers](https://dcyf.wa.gov/services/foster-parenting/kinship-caregivers/roles-responsibilities/payments). {% endCallout %} ## If you get public benefits  Foster care payments might affect your eligibility or benefit amounts for TANF and food stamps. If you list a foster child or youth as a member of your household for eligibility for TANF or food stamps, their foster care payments will count as part of your household income.    You have two options: * Option 1: You can remove the child or youth who gets foster care payments from the household for your benefits eligibility. The state won’t count the payments as household income when it decides your benefits. But your TANF and food stamp benefits will be based on a smaller household size. * Option 2: You can keep the child or youth in the household for your benefits eligibility. The state will count their foster care payments as household income when it decides your benefits.  You can ask your DSHS worker for a “trial eligibility” to explain how each option will affect your benefits. ## Payment levels Foster care payments are given monthly at a flat rate. They are not an itemized reimbursement for individual costs. Each month, you will get an invoice with your estimated monthly payment. The amount is based on the: * Number of children in your care * Number of nights each child was under your care * Payment level we assign you (based on your licensing status and the child or youth's age and physical, developmental, behavioral, or mental health needs) Learn more about [current caregiver payment levels](./payment-levels.html). ### Get payments #### 1. Sign up for the SSPS Provider Portal To simplify the payment process, we use the Social Services Payment System (SSPS) Provider Portal. [Sign up for the SSPS Provider Portal](./payments-ssps.html). #### 2. Sign up for direct deposit (optional) If you’d like your money deposited into your bank account, you can sign up for direct deposit. Using direct deposit is not required, but we recommend it for faster payment. To sign up for direct deposit, you must fill out 2 forms and submit them together:
{% Download "pdf" %}\\[Provider registration form](https://www.dcyf.wa.gov/sites/default/files/forms/ProviderRegistrationForm.pdf){% endDownload %} {% Download "pdf" %}[Direct deposit form](https://www.dcyf.wa.gov/sites/default/files/forms/ProviderDirectDepositAuthorizationform.pdf){% endDownload %}
You have 2 options for submitting the forms:
    {% IconListItem "online" %}E-sign forms [using Docusign](https://ofm.wa.gov/it-systems/accounting-systems/statewide-vendorpayee-services) (fastest method){% endIconListItem %} {% IconListItem "pen" %}Print and sign, then email forms to [ProviderFileUnit@dshs.wa.gov](mailto:ProviderFileUnit@dshs.wa.gov){% endIconListItem %}
{% Callout "note", "undefined" %} It takes 4-6 weeks to process direct deposit forms. We recommend you sign up as soon as possible. {% endCallout %} #### 3. Get your invoice Once you have signed up with the SSPS Provider Portal, DCYF will begin generating invoices for you in the portal. See [sample invoice](https://drive.google.com/file/d/11y2ZBGoWGxWqqNAWOKXIMsCpHgjiI9cj/view?usp=sharing). You have 2 options for how to get invoices:
    {% IconListItem "mail" %}By mail{% endIconListItem %} {% IconListItem "online" %}Electronically, through the SSPS Provider Portal{% endIconListItem %}
SSPS will mail you paper invoices unless you sign up to receive them electronically. To sign up for electronic invoices, choose the “Go paperless” option in the portal. You may opt in to get email notifications when a new invoice is ready. #### 4. Claim your invoice After you get your invoice, you must claim it to get paid. Check that the invoice dates match the dates each child was in your care. Count every night that a child slept in your home. Then claim the invoice to get your payment. {% Callout "note", "undefined" %} We recommend waiting until the last day of the month to claim invoices so they’re accurate. {% endCallout %} You have 3 options for how to claim invoices:
    {% IconListItem "phone" %}[By phone](https://www.dshs.wa.gov/sites/default/files/publications/documents/22-369.pdf): 888-461-8855{% endIconListItem %} {% IconListItem "online" %}Online through [the SSPS Provider Portal](./payments-invoices.html){% endIconListItem %} {% IconListItem "mail" %} By mail: Fill out and sign the invoice. Make a copy for your records. Mail the form to: State of Washington, SSPS PO Box 45889 Olympia, WA 98584-5889 {% endIconListItem %}
{% Callout "note", "undefined" %} If the child left your home before the end of the month the invoice may not reflect this. When invoices are printed, they include estimated stays. You will need to correct it before claiming. {% endCallout %} #### 5. Get your reimbursement Payments are sent the following month. For example, if you have a child in your care in May, you’ll get payment for that care in June. SSPS will mail you a check unless you sign up for direct deposit. If you signed up for direct deposit, you will see the money directly in your account each month. {% Callout "note", "undefined" %} Foster care payments are not income. Do not report them as income to the IRS. They should not affect your taxes but may affect your eligibility for other benefits, like SNAP. {% endCallout %} ### Get help If you have questions, consult the [Provider Payments FAQ](https://www.dcyf.wa.gov/sites/default/files/pdf/ProviderPaymentsFAQ.pdf) or ask the caseworker. {% Callout "related", "undefined" %} * [Other types of financial support](./payments.html) * [Requirements to care for a relative or friend's child](https://dcyf.wa.gov/services/foster-parenting/kinship-caregivers/becoming-kinship-caregiver/placement-requirements) {% endCallout %}
