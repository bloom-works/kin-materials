--- layout: layouts/raw.njk title: Training and services permalink: raw/training-and-services.html templateEngineOverride: md,njk parent_page: Kinship caregiver rules parent_page_link: rules.html --- You can get free training and other services to help meet the child or youth’s needs.  We partner with the [Alliance](https://risewiththealliance.org/), a group of organizations that provides [trainings ](https://dcyf.wa.gov/services/foster-parenting/training)and support to caregivers. Many of the trainings are live or recorded webinars. Some trainings are in-person, group sessions. Visit the[ Alliance website](https://cpe.socialwork.uw.edu/alliance-courses) to create an account and sign up for trainings at any time. For example, you can sign up for [training on how to use positive methods of guidance](https://cpe.socialwork.uw.edu/alliance-courses/content/parenting-positive-discipline-way) to encourage the child or youth to develop self-control, self-responsibility, self-direction, self-esteem, and cooperation.  The Alliance CaRES program provides support groups, peer mentors, and other resources to kinship caregivers. They can also help identify training opportunities. Sign up on the [Alliance CaRES website](https://alliancecares.org/).  If you need extra support to meet the child or youth’s needs, let your caseworker know. We’ll try to identify additional services for you.  {% Callout "note", "Read the regulations" %} For more information read the Washington Administrative Codes (WACs) related to these topics: * WAC 110-149-0070: Training, services, and financial support * WAC 110-149-0130: Meeting the safety and well-being needs of children and youth * WAC 110-149-0270: Discipline * WAC 110-149-0280: Physical restraint {% endCallout %}
