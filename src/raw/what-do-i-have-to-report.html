--- layout: layouts/raw.njk title: What do I have to report? templateEngineOverride: md,njk parent_page: Kinship caregiver rules parent_page_link: rules.html --- You’ll need to report the incidents and changes listed on this page. Read each section to find out how to report. ### Report a missing child or youth If the child or youth is missing, follow these steps: 1. Notify all of these **as soon as you realize that the child or youth is missing (no more than 24 hours later)**: * Law enforcement or tribal law enforcement, and * National Center for Missing and Exploited Children at 1-800-843-5678, and * The child or youth's caseworker (or department intake, if you cannot contact the child or youth’s caseworker directly, or it’s after normal business hours) 2. Provide law enforcement and the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children with: * A recent photo of the child or youth * A description of the child or youth, including: * Height * Weight * Gender * Ethnicity * Race * Hair color * Eye color * Any other health or risk factors, including: * If the the child or youth is pregnant * Their prescription medications * If the child or youth has suicidal tendencies * If the child or youth is vulnerable to being sex trafficked 3. If you find out where the child or youth is, or they return home, report this to the caseworker or department intake. {% Callout "note", "Note" %}Do not give permission to the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children to publicly release information about the child or youth’s health or risk factors.{% endCallout %} ### Report health and safety incidents #### Abuse, harm, or death **Immediately** contact the department intake if your child: * Dies. * Is near death. * Has been [physically or sexually abused, neglected, or exploited](https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=26.44.020) (or if you suspect they have been). #### Physical restraint Contact the child’s caseworker to report behavior that resulted in physical restraint. You must report this **within 24 hours**. This includes restraining a child to save them from harm. #### Medical emergencies Contact the child’s caseworker or the department intake to report medical emergencies. You must report these **within 48 hours**.  Some examples include: * Medicine given or taken wrong. * Serious illness or injury, or psychiatric care. * Disclosed suicidal or homicidal thoughts, gestures, or attempts. * Emergency medical treatment. * Mental health care. #### Sexual contact or behavior Contact the child’s caseworker or the department intake to report concerning sexual contact or sexual behaviors by or towards the child. You must report these **within 48 hours**.  Some examples include: * Non-consensual sexual contact. * Sexual contact that is not age or developmentally appropriate. * Sexual behavior that is not age or developmentally appropriate, such as flashing or public masturbation. ### Report changes in your home or household You must report the changes listed in this section to your licensor. DCYF or the child-placing agency may need you to complete a new or updated assessment when you renew your license. #### Changes in your home Report changes in your home to your licensor **immediately**, including:  * Your address or telephone number has changed. * Your home has structural damage.  * You plan to change the structure of your home.  * You plan to move. #### Household changes Report household changes to your licensor **immediately**, including:  * You have a new spouse or partner. * You separate from your spouse or partner. * Someone who has access to the child or youth is arrested. * A caregiver for the child or youth dies. * Someone moves in or out of the home or property. * You or someone in the household has a change in physical or mental health that interferes with the child or youth’s care. * You experience any medical changes, including changes to your prescription drugs, that affect your ability to care for the child or youth. {% Callout "related", "Related content" %} * Health and medicine * Discipline and restraint * Home study process * Background checks {% endCallout %} {% Callout "note", "Read the regulations" %} For more information read the Washington Administrative Codes (WACs) related to these topics: * WAC 110-149-0290: Reporting incidents * WAC 110-149-0300: Reporting children and youth missing from care * WAC 110-149-0310: Reporting changes in the home {% endCallout %}
