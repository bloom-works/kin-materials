Notify all of these **immediately and within 24 hours**: * Law enforcement or tribal law enforcement, and * National Center for Missing and Exploited Children at 1-800-843-5678, and * The child or youth's caseworker (or department intake, if you cannot contact the child or youth’s caseworker directly, or it’s after normal business hours 2. Provide law enforcement and the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children with this information: * A recent photo of the child or youth * A description of the child or youth's physical features, including: * Height * Weight * Gender * Ethnicity * Race * Hair color * Eye color * Any other health or risk factors, including: * If the the child or youth is pregnant * Their prescription medications * If the child or youth has suicidal tendencies * If the child or youth is vulnerable to being sex trafficked 3. If you find out where the child or youth is, or they return home, report this to the caseworker or department intake. {% Callout "note", "Note" %}Do not give permission to the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children to publicly release information about the child or youth’s health or risk factors.{% endCallout %} ### Report other incidents #### Health and safety Report any of these situations to department intake **immediately:**  * The child or youth dies * The child or youth comes close to dying Report any of these situations **as soon as possible and within 48 hours** to the child or youth’s caseworker (or department intake, if you cannot reach the child or youth’s caseworker, or it’s after business hours): * The child or youth has a serious illness or injury * The child or youth needs psychiatric care * The child or youthtells you they have suicidal or homicidal thoughts * The child or youthmakes suicidal or homicidal gestures or attempts * You gave medicine to the child or youth incorrectly * The child or youth took medicine incorrectly * The child or youth received emergency medical or emergency psychiatric care #### Physical abuse Report any of these situations to department intake **immediately**:  * The child or youth tells you they are being physically abused, neglected, or exploited * You suspect the child or youth is being physically abused, neglected, or exploited #### Sexual contact or abuse Report any of these situations to department intake **immediately**:  * The child or youth tells you they are being sexually abused, or exploited * You suspect the child or youth is being physically or sexually abused, or exploited * Two or more children or youth have sexual contact that’s not consensual or developmentally appropriate according to the American Academy of Pediatrics ### Report changes in your home or household You must report the changes listed in this section to your licensor. DCYF or the child-placing agency may need you to complete a new or updated assessment to get a new license. #### Changes in your home Report any of these situations to your licensor **immediately**:  * Your address or telephone number has changed * Your home has structural damage  * You plan to make changes to the structure of your home  * You plan to move #### Household changes Report any of these changes to your licensor **immediately**:  * You have a new spouse or partner * You separate from your spouse or partner * Someone who has access to the child or youth is arrested. * A caregiver for the child or youth dies. * Someone moves in or out of the home or property. * You or someone in the household experience any serious physical or mental issue that interferes with the child or youth’s care. * You experience any medical changes, including changes to your prescription drugs, that affect your ability to care for the child or youth. {% Callout "related", "Related content" %} * Health and medicine * Reporting serious health and safety incidents * Home study process * Background checks {% endCallout %} {% Callout "note", "Read the regulations" %} For more information read the Washington Administrative Codes (WACs) related to these topics: * WAC 110-149-0290: Reporting incidents * WAC 110-149-0300: Reporting children and youth missing from care * WAC 110-149-0310: Reporting changes in the home {% endCallout %}
