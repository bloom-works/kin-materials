--- layout: layouts/raw.njk title: Home study process templateEngineOverride: md,njk parent_page: Kinship caregiver rules parent_page_link: rules.html --- Before or shortly after a child or youth comes to live with you, DCYF completes a home study. A home study is the process we use to get to know you, and help you prepare to meet the child or youth’s needs. ### Caregiver questions We'll talk with you to learn about you and your family, your plan to care for the child or youth, and how we offer support. Our goal is to make sure you have the support and resources to meet the child or youth’s needs. We’ll discuss these topics:  * The people in your household. * Your relationship with the child or youth and their parents. * How the placement of the child or youth will change your life. * Your plan to meet the child or youth’s health, education, and cultural or religious needs. * How your physical and mental health, substance use, and relationships may affect your ability to care for the child or youth. * Guiding child or youth behavior including [positive discipline](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/discipline-and-restraint/). * [Resources you have, or will need](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/payments), to support the child or youth. ### Physical safety questions We’ll review the physical environment of your home. We’ll identify safety improvements to make or items to get, to meet the child or youth’s needs. {% Callout "note", "Note" %} If you need help, we can support you with ideas and provide any physical items needed to make your home safe. {% endCallout %} We’ll discuss the requirements explained on these home and safety pages: * [Health and medicine](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/healthcare-meds) * [Weapons](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/weapons/) * [Drugs and alcohol](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/drugs-and-alcohol/) * [Transportation](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/transportation) * [Home conditions](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/home-conditions/), including:  * Basic home safety  * Outdoor spaces  * Sleeping spaces * Toxic substances  * Emergency preparedness {% Callout "related", "Related content" %} * [Licensing process](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/licensing-process) * [Background checks](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/background-checks) * [Home conditions](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/home-conditions/) * [What do I have to report?](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/what-do-i-have-to-report) {% endCallout %} {% Callout "note", "Read the regulations" %} For more information read the Washington Administrative Codes (WACs) related to these topics: * WAC 110-149-0150: Home conditions * WAC 110-149-0160: Sleeping arrangements * WAC 110-149-0260: Emergency preparedness * WAC 110-149-0310: Reporting changes in the home {% endCallout %}
