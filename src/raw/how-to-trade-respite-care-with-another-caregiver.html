--- layout: layouts/rawAllCaregivers.njk title: How to trade respite care with another caregiver templateEngineOverride: md,njk --- Licensed foster parents can agree to provide occasional care for each other’s children and youth. The two caregivers can arrange care according to whatever schedule works for them. Trades can be a helpful way to set up care that meets both families’ needs and does not require much involvement from DCYF. **DCYF does not provide payments for caregivers in respite care trades.** Caregivers are allowed to pay each other out of pocket if they choose. If you want to trade respite care with another caregiver, be sure to follow these rules: * Both caregivers have to meet the licensing requirements for the children or youth they will be caring for. For example, if you are only licensed to care for older children and teenagers, you cannot care for a baby in a respite care trade. * Both caregivers must stay within their licensed capacity. For example, if you are licensed to care for four children, you cannot be in charge of five children during a respite trade. * The respite arrangement has to follow the safety plans of all children and youth involved. * If one or both caregivers are licensed through a Child Placing Agency (CPA), they will have to follow any rules that the CPA has for providing respite. * Both caregivers have to get the plan to trade approved by the caseworkers and regional placement desks they work with. * Children and youth can only stay in homes that have been fully licensed through the home study process \[add link].
