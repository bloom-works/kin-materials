--- layout: layouts/raw.njk title: Payments for respite care providers templateEngineOverride: md,njk parent_page: Kinship caregiver rules parent_page_link: rules.html --- Someone who is not the kinship caregiver may be able to help you temporarily care for the child or youth when you need a break. This is called a respite care provider. They could be another relative or a family friend.  If they want to get paid a small stipend from DCYF, they’ll need to complete a certification process. The stipend ranges from [$22.44 to $75 a day](https://www.dcyf.wa.gov/sites/default/files/pdf/fee-respite-foster.pdf), depending on the child or youth’s needs.  {% Callout "note", "Note" %} **Note:** If the person you want to temporarily care for the child or youth does not want the stipend, they do not need to complete the certification process described on this page. Read the page [“Taking breaks from caregiving”](https://www.dcyf.wa.gov/services/foster-parenting/kinship-caregivers/rules/taking-breaks) for more information. {% endCallout %} ### If the person already has a kinship license for other children or youth If the person wants to get paid by DCYF to temporarily care for the child or youth, tell your licensing worker to do a reassessment, and add the child or youth to their license. Respite payments are taxable income. For example, let’s say you care for your niece. Another relative is a licensed kinship caregiver for your niece’s sibling. You can tell your licensing worker you want your relative to temporarily care for your niece sometimes. If your relative is approved, they can get respite payments (different from monthly foster care payments) when they care for your niece.  ### If the person does not have a kinship license If the person wants to get paid by DCYF to temporarily care for the child or youth, they’ll need to meet the requirements in the next section. The payments (also called respite payments) are taxable income. ### Requirements to get paid To get paid for temporarily caring for the child or youth, the person must complete:  * An online application. We'll send the person an email with instructions to access the application in the Washington Caregiver Application Portal (WA CAP).  * An FBI fingerprint-based background check * At least one interview * An Infant Safety Agreement form If you plan for the child or youth to stay at the person’s home, we’ll  also do a [walkthrough of their home](https://dcyf.wa.gov/services/foster-parenting/kinship-caregivers/rules/home-conditions). A DCYF licensing worker will check that the home meets [certain safety requirements](https://dcyf.wa.gov/services/foster-parenting/kinship-caregivers/rules/home-conditions). Everyone in their home who is age 16 and older, and everyone on their property who is age 16 and older who will have unsupervised access to the child or youth must also pass a [background check](https://dcyf.wa.gov/services/foster-parenting/kinship-caregivers/rules/background-checks).  {% Callout "related", "Related content" %} * [Background checks](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/background-checks/) * [Home conditions](https://dcyf-kin-materials.netlify.app/raw/home-conditions/) {% endCallout %} {% Callout "related", "Read the regulations" %} For more information read the Washington Administrative Codes (WACs) related to these topics: * WAC 110-149-0080: Respite or babysitting for children and youth * WAC 110-149-0090: Paid certified kinship respite provider requirements * WAC 110-149-0100: Providing kinship respite care in licensed kinship homes {% endCallout %}
