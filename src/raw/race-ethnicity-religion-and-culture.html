--- layout: layouts/raw.njk title: Race, ethnicity, religion, and culture templateEngineOverride: md,njk parent_page: Kinship caregiver rules parent_page_link: rules.html --- You must provide care that respects your kin child’s race, religion, and culture.  This includes: * Creating a supportive environment for them at home and in your community * Providing opportunities to take part in spiritual, religious, or tribal activities as needed * Sharing relevant resources with them so they can get more support.   Creating a supportive environment could include: * Providing books, media, and toys that reflect your kin child’s background * Including language, food, and traditions from their culture in everyday life You will need to adjust your care based on your kin child's emotional and developmental needs. {% Callout "related", "Related content" %} * LGBTQ+ * Indian children * Food and clothing {% endCallout %} {% Callout "note", "Read the regulations" %} For more information read the Washington Administrative Codes (WACs) related to these topics: * WAC 110-149-0130: Meeting the safety and well-being needs of children and youth {% endCallout %}
