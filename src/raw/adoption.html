--- layout: layouts/rawAllCaregivers.njk title: Adoption templateEngineOverride: md,njk --- As part of our mission to protect children and strengthen families, DCYF strives to safely return a child or youth to a parent. When a child or youth cannot safely return to a parent, and guardianship is not possible, DCYF will support matching children and youth with adoptive families. {% Callout "note", "Note" %} This information is for caregivers of children and youth in DCYF’s custody only. Individual Tribes may have different rules for adoptions of children and youth in the care of a Tribe. If a Tribe has placed a child or youth with you, contact the Tribe, or the Tribal caseworker, for more information on permanency options. {% endCallout %} ## What is adoption? Adoption creates a new legal relationship between the child or youth, and their adoptive family, as if they were born into their adoptive family. The adoptive parent has all care, custody, and legal rights to make decisions for the child.  Before adoption can happen, the court must terminate the rights of the child or youth's parents.  Once the court finalizes an adoption, the child or youth leaves foster care. DCYF is no longer responsible for the child or youth's care. This means:  * The child or youth will no longer have a caseworker who visits monthly.  * DCYF will no longer facilitate visits between the child or youth, and their parents.  * DCYF will no longer reimburse you for mileage or transportation costs.  * You will no longer be able to get concrete goods or services from DCYF.  * The child or youth will no longer be eligible for certain Treehouse services, like free clothing and funding for school activities. * The child or youth’s eligibility for free or reduced lunch at school will be based on your household income.  In practice, it’s almost impossible for parents to regain custody of a child or youth who has been adopted. (This is different from guardianship. In guardianship, it is rare but possible for parents to regain custody in certain situations.)  [Learn more about the responsibilities of adoptive parents](https://dcyf.wa.gov/services/adoption/considering-adoption). ## Who can adopt  If you're a kinship caregiver \[add link], you do not need to be licensed to adopt the child or youth you care for. You may be a kinship caregiver if you care for a relative or friend’s child.  For everyone else interested in adopting from foster care, you must first become a licensed foster parent \[add link].  You’re more likely to be able to adopt a child or youth from foster care if: * You have experience fostering a child or youth * You’re willing to maintain connections with the child or youth’s siblings, relatives and family friends, and culture ## Who’s in need of adoption  These young people have the greatest need for adoptive parents:  * Young people ages 8 through 18  * Sets of siblings  * Young people who have greater emotional, developmental, behavioral, or medical needs  If you specifically want to adopt, you may want to consider fostering a child or youth who has adoption as their approved permanency plan. For a child or youth to be eligible to be adopted, they must be “legally free.” This means a judge must terminate the parents’ legal rights to the child or youth. [Learn more about adoption](https://dcyf.wa.gov/services/adoption). {% Callout "note", "Note" %} DCYF does not complete adoption-only home studies. Working with a private adoption agency may be a better fit for your family if any of these are true:  * You only want to adopt a baby. * You are not open to maintaining family connections.  * You are not open to caring for a child with special needs. {% endCallout %} ## The process of adopting from foster care If you are not a kinship caregiver, you must first become a licensed foster parent to adopt a child or youth from foster care.    The child or youth must live with you as your foster child for at least six months before a court can finalize the adoption. If they are not legally free when they come live with you, the adoption process can take longer while you wait for a judge to terminate parental rights.  Be aware that during the adoption process, DCYF will continue to look for kin who can care for the child or youth. If we find kin at any time before a judge makes the adoption final, we must consider placing the child or youth with them instead.  [Learn more about the process of adopting from foster care](https://dcyf.wa.gov/services/adoption/steps-to-adoption). ## Resources for adoptive children, youth, and families  ### Child care subsidies Adoptive parents may be eligible for up to 12 months of child care subsidies regardless of income. You must apply for the Working Connections Child Care (WCCC) program as a non-needy or in-loco parentis family **before the adoption is finalized**.  After 12 months, you can apply to continue to receive subsidies through WCCC, but you must meet income limits and other eligibility requirements for the program.  [Learn more about child care subsidies through DCYF](https://www.dcyf.wa.gov/services/earlylearning-childcare/getting-help/wccc). {% Callout "note", "Note" %} Working Connections Child Welfare Continuing Child Care is not available for adoptions. {% endCallout %} ### Education Youth who were in foster care any time after they turned 13 years old may be eligible for dedicated financial aid for education after high school, or job training through an apprenticeship program.  [Learn more about educational support for foster youth](https://ospi.k12.wa.us/student-success/access-opportunity-education/foster-care/postsecondary-education-foster-care). ### If you adopt a child or youth with special needs  If you adopt a child or youth with special physical, mental, or emotional needs, your family may be eligible for DCYF’s Adoption Support program. The program can help with certain kinds of financial support and medical services.  [Learn more about DCYF’s Adoption Support program](https://www.dcyf.wa.gov/services/adoption-support-program). {% Callout "related", "Related content" %} * Permanency planning \[add link] * [DCYF’s Guardianship and Adoption Comparison Guide](https://www.dcyf.wa.gov/sites/default/files/pubs/CWP_0117.pdf) * [Adoption Support Program](https://www.dcyf.wa.gov/services/adoption-support-program) {% endCallout %}
