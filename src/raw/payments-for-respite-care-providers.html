--- layout: layouts/rawAllCaregivers.njk title: Payments for respite care providers templateEngineOverride: md,njk --- Respite care providers can receive [small payments for providing care](https://www.dcyf.wa.gov/sites/default/files/pdf/fee-respite-foster.pdf). The amount of the payment varies depending on the situation and the child or youth’s needs. Respite payments are taxable income. You can only get paid for respite care that is arranged ahead of time through the Department of Children, Youth, and Families (DCYF). Licensed foster parents who agree to trade respite care cannot get respite payments through DCYF. ## How to get paid for providing respite care Follow these steps to get paid for providing respite care: * **Step 1:** Get a payment number and a provider number. The caseworker or licensing worker can help with this. You only need to do this step the first time you provide respite care. * **Step 2:** [Sign up for direct deposit](https://sspsmenu.ssps.dshs.wa.lcl/documents/DirectDeposit/ProviderRegistrationForm.pdf) \[update link]. You only need to do this step the first time you provide respite care. * **Step 3:** Confirm that the care you are providing has been arranged through DCYF.  * The child or youth’s caseworker and regional placement desk will confirm that the respite has been scheduled. This usually happens via email. Make sure that the days and times that are confirmed match your plans. * Make sure to confirm your payment rate with DCYF before providing respite care. * **Step 4:** After you provide respite care, follow up with the child or youth’s caseworker and the regional placement desk via email to let them know that the respite is complete. Payments are usually processed within four weeks, but it can take up to six weeks. ## What to do if your payment is late If you have not received your payment after six weeks, email the child or youth’s caseworker and regional placement desk that confirmed the respite. Tell them you have not received the respite payment. If you work with a Child Placing Agency (CPA) or [Caregiver Supports](https://dcyf.wa.gov/services/foster-parenting/caregiver-supports-project/phase-two) placement support manager, you can also contact them for help. If you cannot reach the caseworker, email their supervisor or call the caseworker’s office and ask to speak with their supervisor. You can use the [employee directory](https://apps-ca-ext.dcyf.wa.gov/EmployeeDirectoryInternet/) to find the DCYF caseworker’s supervisor’s contact information or you can look up the [office phone number](https://apps-ca-ext.dcyf.wa.gov/CAOfficesPub/offices/general/OfficePick.asp). If the child or youth you provided care for has a Tribal caseworker, you can also contact the Tribal caseworker for help. ## Taxes on respite care payments Respite care payments from DCYF are taxable income. Because everyone’s tax status is different, the amount of tax withheld from your payment will vary.  If the amount of respite you provide over the course of the year meets tax requirements, you will receive a tax document from DCYF. If you have questions about how to file your taxes, a tax professional can help.
