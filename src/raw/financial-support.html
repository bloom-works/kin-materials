--- layout: layouts/raw.njk title: Financial support templateEngineOverride: md,njk parent_page: Kinship caregiver rules parent_page_link: rules.html --- We understand that the costs of caring for a child or youth can be overwhelming. There are several options for financial support that may be available to you.  ## For licensed foster parents and licensed kinship caregivers only  The main source of financial support for caregivers is [monthly foster care payments](https://dcyf.wa.gov/services/foster-parenting/kinship-caregivers/roles-responsibilities/payments/foster-care-payments). These payments may cover the costs of food, clothing, shelter, and other caregiving expenses.  These payments are available to licensed foster parents and licensed kinship caregivers only.  [Learn more about monthly foster care payments](https://dcyf.wa.gov/services/foster-parenting/kinship-caregivers/roles-responsibilities/payments/foster-care-payments).  {% Callout "note", "Note" %} If you’re considering becoming a kinship caregiver, or you’re already an unlicensed kinship caregiver with DCYF, we encourage you to opt in to a license so you can get monthly foster care payments. You're eligible to get a license after you complete background checks and the home study process. {% endCallout %} ## For licensed foster parents and all kinship caregivers There are some options for financial support for caregivers, regardless of whether you’re a licensed or unlicensed caregiver. These include transportation reimbursement from DCYF, and other types of help from community organizations and Washington state.  Learn more about other types of financial support (add link).
